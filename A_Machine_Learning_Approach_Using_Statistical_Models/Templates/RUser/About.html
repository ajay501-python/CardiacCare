Revolutionizing Neonatal Cardiac Care

Our mission is to reduce infant mortality through early detection of cardiac arrest using advanced machine learning and statistical models

Project Overview

CardiacCare is an innovative research project developing a machine learning system for early detection of cardiac arrest in newborn babies within cardiac intensive care units (CICUs). Our system analyzes real-time physiological data to identify pre-arrest patterns, providing clinicians with critical early warnings.

The project began in 2022 as a collaboration between computer scientists and neonatologists, aiming to reduce the 15-20% mortality rate associated with neonatal cardiac arrest through predictive intervention.

Our Technology

Our system employs a hybrid approach combining:

  • Deep learning models trained on thousands of neonatal cardiac cases
  • Bayesian statistical models for risk probability estimation
  • Real-time vital sign analysis (ECG, SpO2, blood pressure)
  • Adaptive thresholding for patient-specific baselines

The algorithm achieves 82.3% sensitivity in predicting arrest events 3-5 minutes before occurrence, based on clinical trials with 1,200 neonatal patients across 6 hospitals.

Clinical Impact

In preliminary trials, CardiacCare has demonstrated:

  • 38% reduction in unanticipated cardiac arrests
  • 27% improvement in survival rates post-arrest
  • Average 2.8 minute earlier intervention time
  • Reduced neurological complications in survivors

Our system is currently undergoing FDA clearance as a Class II medical device, with planned deployment in 12 children's hospitals by Q3 2024.
